    Advanced study level
    Taught by: Geshe Lobsang Dawa
    Coordination: Ven. Thubten Norbu

The FPMT Basic Program of Buddhist Studies

It was designed by Lama Zopa Rinpoche under the tradition of Lama Je Tsong Khapa, to be taught in five years.

It has been intended for Dharma students who want to advance beyond introductory courses and are interested in committing to deeper study and practice. It offers them the opportunity to develop a good understanding of the whole path to enlightenment and to experience the benefits of a practice-oriented study.

The curriculum consists of nine topics, plus three optional supplements. Root texts and comments are studied, which are complemented with the support of a study group, meditations, retreats, exams, personal study, readings and advice for the practice of each student.

The FPMT will deliver the FPMT Basic Program Certificate to students who successfully complete the global exam and meet all requirements. Candidates for this exam must complete the entire curriculum and three months of Lam Rim's retirement at the end of the program. Likewise, satisfy the criteria of ethical and practical conduct that characterize the values of a true Dharma student.

    What does the Basic Program offer?
- Teachings of the nine topics of the Basic Program.

- Study of the root texts and recommended standard comments.

- The opportunity of exams for each topic.

- The option to prepare for the final exam and the FPMT certificate.

- Lam Rim courses and retreats during the Program.

- Meditation planning, study and continuous debate as integral elements of the courses through the study group.

- Study and practice material for meditation, bibliography, transcription of teachings, schemes and audios.

- Tutoring for study and meditation for enrolled students.
    How to participate:
- Attending specific modules of your interest.

- Attending the entire program and complying with the requirements indicated by the organization.
    Registration in the Basic Program
To get the most out of this Program, it is highly recommended to commit to the study and meditation of root texts. Only enrolled students will be able to receive the study teaching material and will be candidates to receive the Basic Program Diploma if they meet all the requirements that the organization indicates. The study material is distributed for personal use and is subject to copyright, so it may not be copied or reproduced for any purpose. It is possible to register for the entire course or partially for one of its modules at any time. People who want to occasionally attend the Program can also enroll. The contributions requested for the courses are to cover the expenses involved in organizing the Program and maintaining the Center.People with difficulty contributing to the monthly contribution, please contact the Center's management, to see the facilities that may be granted, as well as other forms of collaboration. Your registration is essential to attend classes and to send you study material.

    Mind and Cognition
Introduction to Buddhist knowledge of the mind and its functions. It includes both a presentation of cognition in its various forms, valid and distorted, and Buddhist psychology on the positive and negative mental states relevant to the practice of the path to liberation.
    The Heart Sutra
It reveals the truth of emptiness through a short dialogue between two of the Buddha's most illustrious disciples: Avalokiteshvara and Sariputra. Traditional commentary expands the cryptic style of the sutra to clarify the nature of the wisdom that encompasses emptiness, as well as the practices of the “ method ” that are its essential complement.
    The Wheel of Sharp Weapons
Explanation of the means to develop and increase the Mahayana attitude the aspiration to become a buddha to benefit all beings and, especially, how to transform adverse circumstances to that end.
    Lam Rim (The Stages of the Road)
The gradual path or "Lam Rim" is a famous synthesis of the essential teachings of the Buddha. This module studies Lama Tsong Khapa's medium Lam Rim, which is a commentary on Lama Atisha's "A Light on the Road. Lam Rim's teachings expose the gradual path to Enlightenment from correct devotion to the spiritual master to the wisdom of emptiness for the achievement of Enlightenment.
    Postulates
Based on the idea that the Buddha taught different things according to the abilities of different people, the Tibetan disciples systematized the numerous currents of Indian Buddhist thought in four schools of Postulates, as a means of approaching the deepest philosophical teachings through more accessible levels.
    Bodhisatvacharyavatara (Shantideva)
Commitment to bodhisattva actions "based on Shantideva's inspiring verses on Mahayana aspiration and practice. Composed more than a thousand years ago, they are still widely considered today as the authentic and most complete guide for the practitioner. A classic, offering from simple practices to deal with destructive emotions to the most refined analysis of ultimate truth.
    The Ornament of Clear Realizations
The root text for the study of the levels and types of realization on the path of Enlightenment. Traditionally, it constitutes the basis for the extensive study of study programs in monastic universities.
    The Sublime Continuous Mahayana
One of the most important teachings in all the traditions of Tibetan Buddhism, which clarifies the meaning of our buddhic potential, and especially the emptiness of the mind that allows us to evolve towards the state of full enlightenment.
    Foundations of the Secret Mantra
"Foundations and Paths of the Secret Mantra" gives us a concise overview of the structure of the Tantric Path. A special distinction of Tantra is its unique combination of method and wisdom, achieved through meditating in the perfect form of a completely empty Buddha of real existence.
"Use problems as ornaments, seeing them as extremely precious, because they make you attain enlightenment quickly, by making you attain bodhichitta. Experience these problems on behalf of all sentient beings, giving all happiness to sentient beings. This is the ornament." "Lama Zopa Rinpoche"
